# Project Understanding

## Overview

This project analyzes the impact of Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) on student academic performance using Microsoft Power BI. The objective is to determine whether AI enhances learning outcomes while maintaining healthy study habits, knowledge retention, and student well-being.

The dashboard converts raw educational data into meaningful business insights that support university leadership in making evidence-based academic and AI policy decisions.

---

# Business Problem

Generative AI has become a common learning tool in higher education. However, universities still face several important questions:

- Does AI genuinely improve academic performance?
- Does excessive AI usage increase dependency?
- Does AI reduce knowledge retention?
- Does AI contribute to student burnout?
- Which institutional policies produce the best educational outcomes?

Without analytical evidence, universities risk adopting ineffective or harmful AI policies.

---

# Project Goal

Develop an executive Power BI dashboard capable of:

- Measuring academic performance.
- Monitoring AI adoption.
- Evaluating learning retention.
- Identifying dependency risks.
- Assessing burnout levels.
- Comparing institutional AI policies.
- Supporting strategic decision-making.

---


---

# Key Findings

## 1. AI Improves Academic Performance

Students achieved an average Post Semester GPA of **3.35**, with an average GPA improvement of **0.20** points.

Finding

Responsible AI usage contributes positively to academic performance.

---

## 2. AI Has Become Part of Regular Learning

Students spend an average of **8.43 hours per week** using Generative AI.

Finding

AI has become an important component of modern learning.

---

## 3. Learning Retention Remains Healthy

Students achieved an average Skill Retention Score of **75.80**.

Finding

Students continue retaining knowledge while benefiting from AI-assisted learning.

---

## 4. AI Dependency Remains Low

Only **2.85%** of students reported high AI dependency.

Finding

Most students currently use AI responsibly without excessive reliance.

---

## 5. Burnout Requires Attention

Approximately **24.97%** of students fall into the High Burnout category.

Finding

Student well-being remains an important area for institutional monitoring and support.

---

## 6. Academic Performance Varies Across Majors

Different academic disciplines respond differently to AI adoption.

Finding

Department-specific AI strategies may produce better educational outcomes.

---

## 7. Prompt Engineering Matters

Students with stronger prompt engineering skills generally achieve better academic performance.

Finding

Prompt engineering training can improve AI effectiveness.

---

## 8. Institutional Policies Influence Outcomes

University AI policies affect both academic performance and dependency levels.

Finding

Well-designed AI governance improves educational outcomes.

---

## 9. Balanced AI Usage Produces Best Results

Both underutilization and excessive dependence on AI reduce educational effectiveness.

Finding

Moderate and responsible AI usage provides the greatest academic benefit.

---

## 10. Dashboard Supports Executive Decision-Making

The dashboard enables leadership to identify:

- Academic performance trends.
- Student risk groups.
- Effective AI policies.
- Department performance.
- AI adoption patterns.

Finding

Data-driven decision-making replaces assumptions.

# Project Outcome

The analysis demonstrates that Generative AI can significantly enhance academic performance when used responsibly. Students benefit from improved GPA while maintaining healthy knowledge retention. Although AI dependency remains relatively low, burnout continues to require institutional attention.

The findings suggest that universities should encourage balanced AI adoption supported by effective governance, prompt engineering education, and continuous monitoring of student well-being.

---

# Conclusion

This project demonstrates how Business Intelligence can transform educational data into actionable insights. By combining Power Query, Data Modeling, DAX, and interactive Power BI dashboards, the project provides a complete analytical solution for evaluating the impact of Generative AI on higher education.

The dashboard empowers university leadership to monitor academic success, evaluate AI adoption, identify emerging risks, and make strategic decisions that improve both student performance and long-term learning outcomes.
